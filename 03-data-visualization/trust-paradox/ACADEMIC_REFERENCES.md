# Academic References: The Trust Paradox

## A. Peace Studies & Positive/Negative Peace Framework

### Foundational Texts

1. **Galtung, J.** (1969). "Violence, Peace, and Peace Research". *Journal of Peace Research*, 6(3), 167-191.
   - Seminal definition of positive peace (presence of justice, equity) vs. negative peace (absence of violence)
   - Establishes theoretical distinction underlying our model
   - Cited: Peace indicators framework

2. **Lederach, J. P.** (1997). *Building Peace: Sustainable Reconciliation in Divided Societies*. United States Institute of Peace Press.
   - Frameworks for transforming structural conflict
   - Relationship-building, trust restoration in post-conflict contexts
   - Cited: Institutional capacity for sustainable peace

3. **Braithwaite, J.** (2002). *Restorative Justice and Responsive Regulation*. Oxford University Press.
   - Social trust as alternative to surveillance/coercion
   - Evidence on legitimacy of security institutions
   - Cited: Trust-security trade-off

4. **Rotberg, R. I.** (Ed.). (2004). *When States Fail: Causes and Consequences*. Princeton University Press.
   - Institutional failure and collapse into violence
   - Role of state capacity in maintaining order while preserving legitimacy
   - Cited: Institutional strength dimension

## B. Social Trust & Social Capital

### Core Literature

5. **Putnam, R. D.** (2000). *Bowling Alone: The Collapse and Revival of American Community*. Simon & Schuster.
   - Social capital as aggregate of horizontal trust and civic engagement
   - Relationship between trust and institutional performance
   - Cited: Horizontal vs. vertical trust distinction

6. **Fukuyama, F.** (1995). *Trust: The Social Virtues and the Creation of Prosperity*. Free Press.
   - How high-trust societies develop institutional capacity and economic performance
   - Trust as prerequisite for institutional effectiveness
   - Cited: Trust-institutions interaction

7. **Delhey, J., & Newton, K.** (2005). "Predicting Cross-National Levels of Social Trust: Global Pattern or Nordic Exceptionalism?". *European Sociological Review*, 21(4), 311-327.
   - Cross-national variation in social trust and institutional quality
   - Empirical correlations underlying our model weights
   - Cited: Trust measurement and regional differences

8. **Uslaner, E. M.** (2002). *The Moral Foundations of Trust*. Cambridge University Press.
   - Generalized trust vs. particularized trust
   - Links between inequality and trust erosion
   - Cited: Trust as structural property

9. **Rothstein, B., & Uslaner, E. M.** (2005). "All for All: Equality, Corruption, and Social Trust". *World Politics*, 58(1), 41-72.
   - Institutional corruption as trust-killer
   - Relationship between equality, institutions, and trust
   - Cited: Institutional legitimacy requirement

## C. Institutional Economics & State Capacity

    - Formal and informal institutions
    - Cited: Institutional capacity definition

11. **Acemoglu, D., & Robinson, J. A.** (2012). *Why Nations Fail: The Origins of Power, Prosperity, and Poverty*. Crown Publishers.

    - Connection between institutional quality and long-term stability
    - Cited: Institutions as foundation for sustainable peace

12. **Olson, M.** (1965). *The Logic of Collective Action: Public Goods and the Theory of Groups*. Harvard University Press.
    - How institutions solve collective action problems
    - Relationship between institutional capacity and trust-enabling coordination
    - Cited: Institutional role in enabling cooperation

13. **Evans, P.** (1995). *Embedded Autonomy: States and Industrial Transformation*. Princeton University Press.
    - State capacity and institutional embeddedness in society
    - Balance between autonomy (implementation) and embeddedness (legitimacy)
    - Cited: Institutional strength operationalization

## D. Security, Policing & Legitimacy

### Law Enforcement & Trust

    - How perceived legitimacy of enforcement shapes compliance more than surveillance
    - Trust-based vs. coercion-based compliance
    - Cited: Security measures trade-off

15. **Tyler, T. R.** (2006). "Why People Obey the Law". *Princeton University Press*.
    - Procedural justice and institutional legitimacy
    - Why people accept authority without fear of sanction
    - Cited: Legitimacy as alternative to security overhead

16. **Brinks, D. M., & Coppedge, M.** (2006). "Diffusion Is No Illusion: Neighbor Emulation in the Third Wave of Democracy". *Comparative Political Studies*, 39(4), 463-489.
    - Diffusion of institutional practices and governance models
    - Relevant to understanding regional adoption of security/trust approaches
    - Cited: Cross-national institutional variation

    - Tension between security and human freedom
    - Cited: Surveillance state risks

18. **Zuboff, S.** (2019). *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power*. PublicAffairs.

    - Chilling effects of surveillance
    - Cited: Contemporary security measure risks

## E. Conflict & Peacebuilding Processes

    - Connection to institutional capacity and inequality
    - Cited: Institutional/equity foundations of peace

20. **Fearon, J. D., & Laitin, D. D.** (2003). "Ethnicity, Insurgency, and Civil War". *American Political Science Review*, 97(1), 75-90.
    - Role of state capacity in preventing and ending civil conflict
    - Institutional weakness as risk factor
    - Cited: Institutional strength as violence deterrent

### Peace Processes & Trust-Building

21. **Lederach, J. P.** (2005). *The Moral Imagination: The Art and Soul of Building Peace*. Oxford University Press.
    - Trust restoration in divided societies
    - Role of relationships, legitimacy, and institutions in sustainable peace
    - Cited: Post-conflict trust recovery

22. **Bush, K. D., & Salterelli, D.** (Eds.). (2000). *The Two Faces of Education in Ethnic Conflict: Towards a Peacebuilding Education for Children*. UNICEF & University of Ulster.
    - Education as trust-building institution
    - Institutional role in peace consolidation
    - Cited: Institutions as trust-building mechanisms
