## üéØ The Core Idea (30 seconds)

**The Polarization Spiral** shows that:

1. **Media fragmentation creates silos**  
   ‚ùå People see fewer viewpoints  
   ‚ùå No shared reality across communities

2. **Algorithms amplify extremes**  
   ‚ùå Echo chambers reinforce existing beliefs  
   ‚ùå Radical content gets boosted

3. **Without dialogue, polarization spirals**  
   ‚úÖ Cross-community contact is the antidote  
   ‚úÖ Regular dialogue can reverse the spiral

**Solution**: Maintain media diversity, regulate algorithms, foster genuine dialogue across divides.

## üìä What The Model Measures

Three drivers (0‚Äì100 scale):
- **Media Fragmentation (F)**: How siloed are information sources?
- **Echo Chamber Strength (E)**: How much do algorithms amplify extremes?
- **Cross-Community Dialogue (D)**: How much do communities talk across divides?

Four outputs (0‚Äì100 scale):
- **Polarization Index**: How far apart communities are
- **Information Diversity**: Variety of viewpoints people encounter
- **Cross-Community Understanding**: Empathy and shared knowledge
- **Conflict Likelihood**: Risk of escalation
